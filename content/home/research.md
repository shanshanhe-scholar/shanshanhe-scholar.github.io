### Dissertation: Cognitive Validity of Multimodal L2 Listening Assessment

My dissertation investigates how second language learners process multimodal listening assessments and how test design features shape the cognitive processes elicited by listening tasks. The project combines evidence from research synthesis, EEG, eye tracking, test performance, and learner perceptions.

#### Study 1 — Biometric methods in L2 listening research

A scoping review maps how eye tracking, EEG/ERP, fNIRS, fMRI, and related biometric methods have been used to investigate L2 listening. The review examines study designs, participants, listening tasks, analytical practices, and the contribution of biometric evidence to listening assessment validity.

#### Study 2 — Cognitive engagement in video- and audio-based listening

This EEG study examines whether test takers' cognitive engagement differs across video and audio-only listening conditions and while responding to different item types. Time-frequency measures of brain activity are combined with listening performance, response time, mental-effort ratings, working memory, and proficiency measures.

#### Study 3 — Question-stem access, visual attention, and note-taking

This eye-tracking study investigates how access to question stems influences learners' visual attention, note-taking, task performance, and perceptions during video-based academic listening. It compares listening and answering processes under question-access and no-access conditions.

### Additional research

- **Interactive video in listening assessment:** Examining how embedded questions, segmentation, and feedback influence test-taker experience, performance, and construct representation.
- **Language test preparation:** Synthesizing evidence on preparation practices and their relationships with test performance and language proficiency.
- **Teacher education:** Exploring language assessment literacy, digital identity texts, translanguaging, and the preparation of disciplinary teachers to support multilingual learners.
